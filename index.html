About Me

I am a PhD researcher in Electrical Engineering at the Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST), working at the intersection of computer architecture and AI systems. My research focuses on making AI computationally viable at scale — through adaptive concurrency for LLM agent frameworks and near-data processing with computational SSDs.

My dissertation, Efficient AI Systems: From Computational Storage to LLM Agent Optimization, reflects a conviction that the future of AI lies not only in better models, but in dramatically more efficient systems around them.

ATCP — Adaptive Tool-Aware Concurrency for LLM Frameworks Under Review · ICML 2026
15× throughput 13× energy reduction/query 86–91% GPU utilization NVIDIA RTX A6000
  • Designed an adaptive concurrency optimizer overlapping LLM inference with tool execution across independent agent sessions using a two-phase control algorithm (workload profiling + PID-based feedback).
  • Achieved 15× throughput improvement and 13× energy reduction per query while sustaining 86–91% GPU utilization without modifying model internals.
SeeSSD — Computational Storage for Energy-Efficient Object Detection Published · ACM TECS 2026
38.1% mAP on PASCAL VOC 1.2–1.4× power reduction 24× less data movement 0.21s inference latency
  • Designed an FPGA-based computational SSD performing CNN inference within the storage device; developed YOLO_Embed, a 9-layer lightweight CNN.
  • Reduced power consumption 1.2–1.4× over CPU/GPU baselines and data movement by 24× per image through in-storage near-data processing.
